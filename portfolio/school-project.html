School Project

Educational | Abu Dhabi, UAE | 2024

The School Project represents a forward-thinking approach to educational architecture that draws inspiration from traditional Arabic design principles while incorporating modern sustainable technologies. The campus is designed to create an optimal learning environment that responds to the local climate while fostering community and collaboration.

The layout is organized around a series of courtyards that provide shaded outdoor learning spaces and facilitate natural ventilation throughout the buildings. These courtyards feature indigenous plantings and water elements that create microclimates and serve as living laboratories for environmental education.

Building facades incorporate contemporary interpretations of mashrabiya screens that filter harsh sunlight while allowing for views and ventilation. The material palette includes locally-sourced stone and earth-toned plaster that minimize heat gain while connecting the architecture to its regional context.

Sustainability Features

Passive Solar Design

Building orientation and shading elements are optimized to minimize heat gain while maximizing natural daylight, reducing the need for artificial lighting and cooling.

Water Conservation

Greywater recycling systems and rainwater harvesting provide water for landscape irrigation and toilet flushing, while low-flow fixtures reduce overall water consumption.

Renewable Energy

Rooftop photovoltaic panels generate clean electricity, meeting approximately 40% of the school's energy needs, while solar thermal collectors provide hot water.
